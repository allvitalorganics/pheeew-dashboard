MOQ STRUCTURE:
Pouch printing = 120K per SKU design per roll. Each SKU has its OWN roll.
Filling = 40K per SKU minimum.
So: 1 SKU at 40K fill = 40K filled pouches + 80K empty pouches remaining on that SKU's roll.

THE POUCH ROLL COST IS INCLUDED IN THE PER-UNIT PRICE. The manufacturer's quoted price already includes the pouch. You do NOT pay separately for the roll. The 80K remaining pouches sit at the manufacturer waiting for your next production run.

The risk: The 80K empties become YOUR cost ONLY if you never manufacture that SKU again. If you do a second run, they're pre-paid — the per-unit cost on run 2 drops because the pouches are already printed and waiting. The manufacturer just fills them.

Dictionary

Every term the team needs to know — from zero to operational

PRODUCT & SCIENCE

Synbiotic

A product that contains both PREbiotics (food for good bacteria) AND PRObiotics (live good bacteria). Pheeew is synbiotic. Most brands are only one or the other.

Probiotic

Live microorganisms that provide health benefits when consumed in adequate amounts. Our probiotic: Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969.

Prebiotic

Non-digestible fibers that feed the good bacteria already in your gut. Our prebiotics: XOS + Fibersol-2L.

Spore-forming

A type of bacteria that creates a protective shell (spore) around itself. Survives heat, shelf storage, and stomach acid. Most probiotics are NOT spore-forming and die before reaching the gut. B. coagulans IS spore-forming — this is our key differentiator.

CFU (Colony Forming Units)

How probiotic quantity is measured. "1 Billion CFU" = 1 billion live bacteria per serving. Must be measured at END of shelf life, not at manufacture.

XOS (Xylo-oligosaccharides)

A type of prebiotic fiber derived from plant material. Feeds beneficial gut bacteria.

Fibersol-2L

A soluble prebiotic fiber (resistant maltodextrin) made by Matsutani/ADM. Clinically studied for digestive health. Liquid form designed for beverages.

RCT (Randomized Controlled Trial)

The gold standard of clinical research. Participants randomly assigned to treatment or placebo. Our probiotic has 4+ RCTs published. This is what "clinically studied" means.

Puree vs Juice

Puree = thick, made from whole fruit pulp. Juice = thin, water-based, filtered/diluted. Pheeew uses PUREE format (April 14 decision). Thicker mouthfeel, more flavor, slightly higher COGS.

BUSINESS & FINANCE

COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)

What it costs to make one unit. Our manufacturing COGS (confirmed): avg ₩1,333/$0.98 per pouch (120K pricing). Landed COGS (including shipping to US): ~₩1,533/$1.13 per pouch. Source: 좋은영농조합법인 견적서 2026-04-06.

Gross Margin

(Revenue - COGS) ÷ Revenue. Ours: ~62% (subscription 30-pack), ~75% (one-time). Investors want 50%+ for supplements. We exceed that.

CM1 (Contribution Margin 1)

Gross profit minus variable costs (shipping, payment processing, returns). Ours: ~47% (sub), ~64% (one-time). This is profit BEFORE marketing.

CM2 (Contribution Margin 2)

CM1 minus marketing and content costs. Ours: ~20% (sub), ~34% (one-time). This is operating profit per unit.

ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)

Total subscription revenue projected over 12 months. 1,000 subscribers × $89/month × 12 = $1.07M ARR.

CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

How much you spend to get one new customer. Industry average for DTC supplements: $45-53. Our target: <$35 (founder-led content reduces this).

LTV (Lifetime Value)

Total revenue from one customer over their lifetime. If a subscriber stays 12 months: $1,068 LTV (30-pack).

PMF (Product-Market Fit)

When people want your product enough to buy it repeatedly without heavy marketing. Signal: 30%+ repeat purchase rate at 90 days.

MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)

Smallest amount a manufacturer will produce. Our fill MOQ: 40,000 units per flavor. Pouch printing MOQ: 120,000 pouches.

DTC (Direct-to-Consumer)

Selling directly through your own website, not through retailers. Highest margin channel. You own the customer data.

MSRP (Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price)

The price consumers see. One-time: $4.50/pouch. Subscription: from $2.80/pouch (60-pack) to $3.00/pouch (15-pack). 30-pack sub: $89/month ($2.97/pouch).

INVESTMENT

SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)

The most common investment instrument for pre-seed startups. Not a loan. Investor gives money now, gets equity later when you do a priced round. No interest, no maturity date. Y Combinator created it.

Valuation Cap

The maximum company value at which a SAFE converts to equity. If cap = $2M and investor put in $200K, they get at least 10% equity regardless of how high the next round values the company.

Pre-money Valuation

Company value BEFORE investment money comes in. Post-money = pre-money + investment. Example: $1.5M pre-money + $150K investment = $1.65M post-money.

Dilution

When new shares are issued (for investors), existing shareholders own a smaller percentage. Normal and expected. CPG pre-seed: 12-18% dilution is standard.

Angel Investor

Individual person who invests their own money in early-stage companies. Typical check: $25K-$100K for food/bev. Different from VCs who invest other people's money.

Convertible Note

Like a SAFE but structured as a loan. Has interest rate (typically 5%) and maturity date (typically 24 months). More familiar to Korean investors.

LEGAL & REGULATORY

Conventional Food

What Pheeew is classified as. Different from "dietary supplement." Uses Nutrition Facts panel, not Supplement Facts. No FDA notification needed for claims. No disclaimer needed.

Class 32 (Trademark)

The trademark classification for non-alcoholic beverages. This is what Pheeew's trademark is filed under.

GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe)

FDA designation meaning an ingredient is safe for use in food. Our probiotic (B. coagulans SNZ 1969) has GRAS status — FDA GRN 597 and 864.

Structure/Function Claim

"Supports digestive health" — describes how an ingredient affects body structure or function. Legal on conventional food WITHOUT FDA notification or disclaimer.

Disease Claim

"Prevents IBS" — claims to treat, prevent, or cure a disease. ILLEGAL on food or supplements. Makes your product a drug. Never say this.

FTC (Federal Trade Commission)

Regulates advertising claims. Everything you say on social media, ads, and website must be truthful and substantiated. Same standard as packaging.

FDA (Food and Drug Administration)

Regulates food labeling, packaging, and safety. Pheeew needs FDA facility registration (free) for the US manufacturer/importer.

MANUFACTURING

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer)

A company that manufactures products for other brands. 좋은영농조합법인 is our OEM — they make the smoothie, we sell it under our brand.

ODM (Original Design Manufacturer)

Like OEM but they also design/formulate the product. Our manufacturer handles both formulation and production.

Landed COGS

Total cost of getting one unit from factory to your US warehouse. Includes: manufacturing + packaging + domestic freight + sea freight + customs + duty + broker + 3PL receiving. Our landed COGS: ~$1.13/unit (120K confirmed) / ~$1.17/unit (40K confirmed).

3PL (Third-Party Logistics)

A warehouse company that stores your inventory, picks/packs orders, and ships to customers. You don't own the warehouse — you pay per order fulfilled.

FCL (Full Container Load)

A full shipping container from Korea to US. 40ft container = ~60,000+ pouches. Cheaper per unit than LCL (Less than Container Load).

EXW (Ex-Works)

Pricing term meaning "price at the factory door." Doesn't include shipping, customs, or duties. Our manufacturer quotes EXW.

MARKETING

UGC (User-Generated Content)

Content created by customers or creators, not the brand. A customer filming themselves drinking Pheeew = UGC. Best-performing ad creative format.

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)

Revenue generated per dollar spent on ads. $3 ROAS = $3 revenue for every $1 spent. Target: 3x+ for sustainable DTC.

Spark Ads (TikTok)

Boosting organic TikTok posts as paid ads. Uses real creator content with their handle. Higher trust than traditional ads.

Seeding

Sending free product to creators/influencers without requiring a post. They post if they genuinely like it. Lower cost, higher authenticity than paid sponsorships.

Velocity (Retail)

How fast a product sells per store per week. Units Per Week (UPW). <2 UPW = dying on shelf. 5-10 = solid. 20+ = category leader. Retailers use this to decide if you stay.

CPM (Cost Per Mille/Thousand)

How much you pay per 1,000 ad impressions. Average ecommerce CPM: $14-18. Q4 (holiday): $25-35. Lower CPM ≠ better — high CTR matters more.

CTR (Click-Through Rate)

% of people who see your ad and click it. Above 1% = healthy. Below 0.8% after 1K impressions = kill the creative.

CPC (Cost Per Click)

How much each click on your ad costs. Derived from CPM ÷ CTR × 10. Lower = more efficient ads.

Hook Rate

% of viewers who watch past the first 3 seconds of a video. Above 30% = good hook. Below 20% = rewrite the opening.

Thumb-Stop

Content that makes someone stop scrolling. The first frame of every video or the headline of every ad. If it doesn't stop thumbs, nothing else matters.

RPR (Revenue Per Recipient)

How much revenue one email generates per person who receives it. Welcome flow: $1.50-4/recipient is healthy. Below $1 = flow is broken.

Flow (Klaviyo)

Automated email sequence triggered by customer behavior (signup, purchase, cart abandonment). Flows run 24/7 without manual work. Different from campaigns (one-time blasts).

Campaign (Klaviyo)

One-time email blast to a segment of your list. Weekly newsletters, product launches, sales. Different from flows (automated).

Segment (Email)

A group of subscribers filtered by behavior. "Engaged 30 days" = opened email recently. "Never purchased" = leads. "Purchased 2+" = VIPs. You send different content to different segments.

RETAIL

Slotting Fee

One-time payment to get your product on a retailer's shelf. $5K-$25K per SKU at major chains. Natural/specialty retailers often don't charge. This is why you start at indie stores.

UPW (Units Per Week)

How many units sell per store per week. The #1 metric retailers care about. Below 2 UPW = getting cut. 5-10 = solid. 20+ = category leader.

Endcap

The display at the end of a store aisle. Premium placement = more sales. Brands pay for endcap placement through trade spend.

Planogram

The visual map of where products sit on a shelf. Retailers design planograms. Your position (eye level vs bottom shelf) dramatically affects sales.

Trade Spend

Money you pay retailers for promotions, placement, and marketing. Can be 20-30% of gross revenue for retail brands. Includes: slotting, promos, free fills, demo days.

Deductions

Money retailers subtract from your invoice for various fees (spoilage, damaged goods, promotional allowances, data fees). UNFI/KeHE deductions can eat 25-35% of your wholesale price.

Free Fill

First order of product given free to a retailer to stock their shelves. Common requirement for new brands entering retail. Budget for this.

Broker

A third-party who represents your brand to retail buyers. Takes 3-5% commission. Helpful when you don't have relationships with buyers. Not needed for DTC.

AMAZON

FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon)

You ship inventory to Amazon's warehouse. They store, pick, pack, and ship to customers. You get the Prime badge. Fees: referral (8-15%) + fulfillment (~$5-7/unit).

FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant)

You store and ship products yourself (or through your 3PL). No Prime badge. Lower fees but lower conversion rate.

SFP (Seller Fulfilled Prime)

You ship from your warehouse but earn the Prime badge. Must meet strict delivery and performance requirements. Best margin + visibility combo.

ACoS (Advertising Cost of Sales)

Ad spend ÷ ad revenue. 20% ACoS = you spent $20 in ads for every $100 in ad-attributed sales. Target: below 25% for established products.

TACoS (Total Advertising Cost of Sales)

Ad spend ÷ TOTAL revenue (including organic). More important than ACoS. Shows if ads are building organic momentum. Target: 8-12% for established products.

Buy Box

The "Add to Cart" button on an Amazon listing. Multiple sellers can list the same product, but only one gets the Buy Box. Lose it = revenue drops to near-zero.

IPI (Inventory Performance Index)

Amazon's score for how well you manage inventory. Below 400 = storage limits. Above 400 = healthy. Running out of stock kills your IPI and ranking.

MANUFACTURING

Batch

One production run of a specific recipe. A batch of 40,000 pouches = one continuous run. Different batches may have slight variations.

Lot Number

Unique identifier for each production batch. Required for traceability. If there's a recall, you need to know which lot is affected.

Shelf Life

How long the product stays safe and maintains quality. For Pheeew: TBD from manufacturer. CFU must be guaranteed at END of shelf life, not manufacture date.

Fill Weight

The exact amount of liquid in each pouch. Must be consistent. Underfilling = legal issue (misbranded). Overfilling = lost margin.

Brix

Measurement of sugar content in a liquid. Higher Brix = sweeter. Used by manufacturers to ensure consistency between batches.

Spec Sheet

Document listing every detail about your product: ingredients, allergens, nutritional info, packaging dimensions, shelf life, storage requirements. Retailers and distributors require this before considering your product.

FINANCE / INVESTMENT

Burn Rate

How much cash you spend per month. If you have $50K and burn $10K/month, you have 5 months of runway.

Runway

Cash in bank ÷ monthly burn rate = months before you run out of money. 12-18 months runway is ideal when raising.

Cap Table

The spreadsheet showing who owns what % of the company. Alex 35%, Jason 33%, Dawon 32% = your cap table before any outside investment.

Pro Rata

An investor's right to maintain their ownership % in future funding rounds by investing proportionally. Standard in most investment agreements.

Anti-Dilution

Protection for investors if the company raises money at a LOWER valuation later ("down round"). Adjusts their shares to compensate. Common in VC term sheets, less common in angel/SAFE deals.

MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio)

Total revenue ÷ total marketing spend. Ignores attribution. If MER is trending up, your overall marketing is working. The cross-channel sanity check.

Down Round

Raising money at a lower valuation than your previous round. Bad signal. Means the company is worth less than before. Try to avoid by raising at reasonable valuations early.

Term Sheet

A non-binding document outlining the key terms of an investment deal. Covers: amount, valuation, equity %, investor rights, board seats. You negotiate the term sheet before signing final legal docs.
