01Company Overview

Product and Positioning

Pheeew is a 100mL synbiotic puree pouch, designed for daily gut support. Not juice, not a shot, not a bottle. The category has been owned by prebiotic sodas and premium HPP juices. Pheeew occupies the open lane: pouch format plus a full synbiotic stack (prebiotic fiber plus probiotic).

02Market Opportunity

Category and Competitive Landscape

Gut health is a $50B+ US TAM growing 14% annually. Synbiotic search terms are up 22% year over year. Every funded competitor sells in a bottle or a can. The pouch lane is uncontested at scale, and synbiotic (pre + pro) is the functional wedge.

Market Size

$50B+ TAM

US gut health category, 2024. Functional beverage compound growth: 14% CAGR through 2028. Synbiotic segment up 22% search YoY.

Competitive Set

Bottles and cans.

Poppi (~$150M val), Olipop (~$1.85B val), Suja premium HPP, Culture Pop. All either prebiotic-only or sugar-heavy. None in pouch format.

Our Wedge

Pouch × synbiotic.

Format differentiation redefines occasion (throw in a bag, drink in 10 seconds). Synbiotic is the functional advantage. We are not a soda alternative, we are gut support in smoothie form.

04Go-to-Market

Six-Month Launch Plan

Production lead time at the factory is 3 to 4 months. If we test demand first and commit afterward, launch slips by half a year. Instead, we commit the 40K MOQ at funding close, then run LA farmer's markets with a small pre-production sample batch during the wait. Main inventory lands Week 16 and full DTC launch begins.

A · Deploy
Week 1–4
Close round. Wire factory deposit for Wild Berries 40K. Order pouches (120K MOQ). Alex and Dawon fly LA. Pre-production sample batch (500–1,000 pouches) air-shipped.
B · Test
Week 5–16
Two farmer's markets per week. Pre-order page live. Creator seeding wave 1 (30 creators). Signal targets: ≥500 waitlist, ≥100 pre-orders, ≥150 pouches/wk at market, >15% repeat rate.
C · Launch
Week 16–20
40K ocean shipment lands LA. 3PL intake. Full DTC launch. Subscription stack live (Recharge). Scaled paid media. First subscription cohort.
D · Decide
Week 24–26
Data-driven review with CFO advisor. Go or no-go on Phase 2 SKU (Tropical Punch). Seed round prep kickoff if signal is strong.

LA deployment: Alex and Dawon on the ground for 8 to 10 weeks. Housing and car provided by Jason (zero line items). All other costs itemized in the budget.

12Risk Analysis

Risk Register and Mitigants

Top five risks from the internal register. Full nine-row register sits in the financial model. Each mitigant is either a budget line item or a process commitment, not a hope.

Formulation fails lab test (shelf-life, microbial)
Mitigant: $4K reformulation reserve budgeted. Lab testing occurs before commit of main production run.
Farmer's market demand signal is soft
Mitigant: Test runs during the production lead time, not after. 40K inventory can still launch DTC even if market is soft. Kill-switch review at Week 24.
Factory production delay (capacity, ingredients)
Mitigant: Order placed Week 1. Weekly factory check-ins. Sample batch gives early visibility. Contingency freight line item.
Founder capacity (all three part-time at start)
Mitigant: $35K stipend line item funds partial full-time for 6 months. Full-Time Threshold clause in Co-Founder Agreement.
Family investor governance dynamic
Mitigant: Priced round with standard terms. Written advisory agreement. Jason's COO role creates alignment anchor. No oral agreements.
