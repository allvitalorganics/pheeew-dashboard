01 / reality Three things to internalize before planning corrected from earlier drafts

The locked facts.

!

Naju MOQ is 40,000 per SKU. No pilot. No exceptions.

Naju (좋은영농조합법인) production MOQ is firm at 40K. Their pouch material MOQ is 120K (with safety cap, carries to next batch). Anyone telling us a "10K pilot" is misreading the quote — it doesn't exist.

!

Full launch = 3 SKUs at MOQ each = 120,000 pouches

When we trigger Naju, all three flavors go at once — Berry Mix · Pear Ginger · Tropical Punch, 40K each. Not a phased rollout from the factory. The phases in the brand roadmap (Phase 1 anchor, Phase 2, Phase 3) refer to what we lead with in marketing, not what comes off the production line. Production is one big run.

i

Commissary kitchen is for validation only — and scale-readiness in parallel

$20K can't fund Naju. It can fund ~6,000 pouches in commissary hot-fill batches for farmers markets + DTC validation, while the same $20K covers all the licenses, registrations, broker setup, and insurance we'll need on day one of the Naju run. The investment is in two things: validation (right now) and infrastructure (for whenever the trigger comes).

i

Per-unit landed at full launch is the actual unit economics

$1.07 per pouch landed on a $3.40 blended DTC MSRP = 69% gross margin. After ShipBob pick/pack (~$0.40) + payment processing (~3%) = ~52% contribution margin. That's the math that makes paid acquisition viable, that supports wholesale margins, and that justifies the $130K commitment. Validation-mode commissary economics ($2.50 COGS, 26% gross margin) only work for organic farmers-market sales, not scale.

04 / buyer scenario "A wholesale buyer wants to talk." 14–20 wk if scale-ready · 24+ wk if not

When the phone rings.

A buyer (Erewhon, Whole Foods, Sprouts, Gelson's, Bristol Farms, regional independents) sees the brand at a market or online and wants to talk wholesale. Whether the deal happens depends on what we already have in place when they ask.

Day 0 Buyer email · "we'd like to discuss carrying you"

Standard opener. Buyer has either tasted us at a market, seen us on Instagram, or heard about us from a broker. They'll ask three things: do you have product · what's your wholesale price · can you commit to a delivery date. Our answer to all three needs to be ready before the email arrives.

What they ask
  • Cases on hand? "How much can you ship this month?"
  • Wholesale price? "Your case price for X units?"
  • Lead time? "Reorder window if we move volume?"
  • Compliance? "FDA registered? Insurance? Recall plan?"
  • Logistics? "EDI / 3PL / shipping terms?"
What we need ready
  • Wholesale price tiers built (Naju COGS-based)
  • Sample case pulled from commissary inventory
  • Insurance certificate copy on file
  • FDA Food Facility Registration #
  • Sell sheet PDF · COA from lab
  • UPC/GTIN registered (GS1)

Week 1 Sample sent · negotiation

Buyer tastes, decides, comes back with terms. Standard ask: Net 30 payment, slotting fee waived, demo budget shared, 90-day fill-rate guarantee. Our job: don't say yes to anything we can't fulfill. The biggest trap is committing to volume we can't ship.

If they want <5K units / month
  • Possibly fillable from commissary inventory if we've stockpiled
  • Naju trigger NOT necessarily fired
  • Use the deal to fund the next commissary batch
If they want ≥10K units / month
  • Naju trigger fires — this is the moment
  • 50% deposit ($65K) due within 7 days of PO
  • 14–20 weeks to landed inventory
  • Buyer must accept that timeline · push for advance PO + commitment

Week 2 → 4 Decision point · trigger or pass

If we trigger Naju, we burn the bridge with a $65K commit. If we pass, the buyer moves to a competitor. The decision pivot: can we secure 50% advance payment from the buyer to fund our 50% deposit to Naju? Some retailers will, most won't — but it's worth asking. Otherwise the trigger requires our own capital or a funding round.

Trigger fires · Day 1 of Naju
  • Naju PO sent + Origin Cert clause + KORUS preference
  • 50% deposit wired ($65K)
  • Pouch art final to Naju
  • Customs broker confirmed for the entry
  • ShipBob warned: "120K pouches inbound week 18"
Trigger doesn't fire · alt path
  • Pass on the deal · stay in commissary mode
  • Tell the buyer "Q2 next year" or specific date
  • Use their interest as funding-round signal
  • Keep the relationship warm
